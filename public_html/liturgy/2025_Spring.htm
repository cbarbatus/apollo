Mike: Salvete Grovies and guests! We gather tonight to honor the gods of Ancient Rome who live with us and provide for us.

Liberalia in the city included a procession of wine, honey, and cakes. In the countryside, women carried a phallus throughout the countryside to bring the blessing of fertility to the land and the people, and to protect the crops from evil. At the end of the procession, a virtuous and respected matron placed a wreath upon the phallus. Our women tonight are carrying a spear instead, but you get the idea.

Some elements tonight are translations of actual Ancient Roman practice.

Honoring the Sacred Flame

Mike: Druids, the goddess Vesta is the keeper of the hearth and symbolizes purity, unity, and the warmth and illumination of the sacred eternal flame that represents the heart of our worship. At the same time, Vesta [Wess-tah] represents the heart of humanity, connecting us to our past. She represents the warmth and illumination of the sacred fire.

Setting Historical Precedence Statement

Mike: The festival of Liberalia was held in honor of Liber Pater, Father Freedom, a fertility god whose worship dated from nearly 200 years after the founding of Rome, which is about 500 years before Julius Caesars time. Liber Pater, along with his female equivalent and partner Libera and the earth goddess Ceres, shared a temple on the Aventine Hill in Rome. Liberalia, it is said, was celebrated with sacrifices, crude songs, processions, and liberties of all sorts.

As we have gathered in this sacred space, let us now center and prepare ourselves for this rite and its work.

Statement of Ritual Purpose

Amiel: We are here to honor the gods. So, let us pray with a good fire. Let us offer with a full heart knowing that when we do, we nurture our relationship with the Kindreds. In remembering, we strengthen and sustain the gods themselves. In honor, we balance the worlds, and in love we bring peace and keep the Old Ways alive and present.

Mike: Here tonight we honor the gods. We reassure them that, though millennia have passed, they are not forgotten. We pay special homage tonight to the triad who were revealed in 496 BC by the Sibylline Books to be worshipped and thereby end a terrible famine. To bring fertility, enjoyment, and abundance tonight, we honor Liber Pater, Libera, and Ceres.
