Warrior will make the offering to the sea.

Ritual Purpose

 

Priest 1: “Hermanos y Hermanas! Brothers and Sisters of the Old Ways! We stand here together on the border between land and sea and under the light of the Sun to celebrate the turning of Summer. This is a time of vitality, movement, and passion. A time of heat, light, and fire. We are also here to celebrate the bright and shining energies within ourselves. We are here to celebrate our tradition and our dedication to the Gods by honoring Lugus and Nabia. We welcome each and every one of you here with open hearts and arms. Let us celebrate this time together as Family!”

Establishing the Sacred Center/Hallow (Fire)

 

 

Drummers: ¾ beat (Optional)

 

Three Priests stand around the fire pit each holding an unlit torch. The Priestess presents a candle from which the three torches are lit.

 

Priest 1: “As all life revolves around the great flame in the sky so too will our lives revolve around the flame that is established here. From this center will we worship and make offering to our Gods and Kindred. May it be strong and bright and worthy of their attention!”

 

The three-lit torches are held up to the sky.

 

“Incendio sagrado, quema con fuerza en nuestros corazones!”

“Sacred Fire, burn with strength in our hearts!”

 

 

The torches are thrown into the fire pit and the central fire is lit.

 

“Teiuoreikis!”

 

 

Priest 1: “As we have prepared the center, let us now prepare ourselves for the welcoming of the gods.”

 

Centering

 

“Close your eyes and breathe deeply.”

(Pause)

 

“Turn your mind and your hearts to the Sea. Listen to the movement of the waves. Allow the moist air to fill your being in the understanding that the waters in your body are the same as those in the great Ocean that connect the worlds and surrounds you now.”

“Listen.”

 

“Breathe Deeply.”

 

(Pause)

 

“Now, lift your minds and faces to the Sun. Feel the warmth of the sacred Day Star on your skin. Allow the heat to sink into you and warm your being in the understanding that the heat in your own body is the same as that of the Sun that shines above you now.” “Feel.”

 

(Pause)

 

“Breathe deeply.”

 

(Pause)

 

“Now, focus inward. Listen and feel for your own heartbeat. (Pause) The blood that courses in your veins is the very energy of both the Sea and the Sun. It is both the water and the fire in your body. Its pulse is the very rhythm of Life inside and all around you.” “Listen…..feel.“

 

(Pause)

 

“As you slowly open your eyes, continue to listen to and feel all the sounds of the Life that surrounds you. The people, the traffic, the sea. ”

Priest 1: “We will now prepare to welcome the gods by honoring the Earth Mothers.”

 

Honoring the Mothers

 

Drummer: Heart Beat Rhythm.

 

Priest:  “Sacred and powerful Earth Mothers. Your compassion and bounty sustain and give us life. We honor your presence and acknowledge our reliance on you as your children, for without you we would not live.”

 

Priest will dig three small holes in the sand at various points in the circle and pour the libation into each hole as he or she speaks 3 times:

 

(Gently spoken)

“Huius Matronibus, accepta la ofrenda de tus hijos.”

 

Once all three libations have been poured, the priest returns to the center of the circle and kneels then raises the vessel to the heavens and pours out what is left onto the sand before them as he/she speaks:

 

“Mothers of this place, accept the offering of your children!”

(All repeat)

 

“Teiuoreikis!”

 

 

 

Opening of the Gates

Priest 1: “Let us now prepare ourselves to open the gates between the worlds, so that the Gods may better hear our voices.”

 

Priestess: Danza de Endovellico. (Dance of Endovellico)

 

This is a layered magical act involving a dancer, drummer, and priest (orator). The drummer plays a traditional Spanish 3-count beat that progressively increases in speed as the dance intensifies and also increases in speed. As the energy builds, the Priest begins the invocation of Endovellico while holding a libation. Bards will encourage the people to clap along in rhythm in order to raise energy and assist in the opening of the gates. Note: The orator and dancer must work in coordination. The dancer will set the pace for this act and the orator must follow her energy. Only when the dancer’s energy is at its peak, will the orator begin his/her invocation and offering.

 

 

Invocation: Priest 2 “Father of Dreams! Possessor of sacred mysteries. Guardian of the gates between worlds. We call on you to open the boundaries between our people and the Gods, so that we may be heard!”

 

Priest walks three times around the fire pit and chants:

 

Endovellico! Abre los portillos!

 

 

Endovellico! Abre los portillos!

 

 

Endovellico! Abre los portillos!

 

 

Priest comes to a stop, holds the libation cup up high then throws the libation into the fire and says:

 

“Endovellico! Let the gates be opened!”

(All repeat)

“Teiuoreikis!”

 

 

Priest 1: “The gates have been opened. Let us now make offering to our Kindred.”

“Antepasados, accepta nuestra offrenda!”

 

“Ancestors, accept our offering.”

(All repeat)

 

“Teiuoreikis!”

 

 

 

Deities (Fire)– “Bright and Shining Ones above us, dark and mysterious ones below us. You who inspire and challenge us. We are here to honor your strength and ask that you continue to be powerful examples for our lives.”

 

“Deidades, accepta nuestra offrenda!”

 

“Deities, accept our offering.”

(All repeat)

Main Rite/Gods of Honor

 

 

Priest 1: “As we have made offering to the Kindred, let us now honor the Gods of this rite.”

 

 

Priest: “ We will now honor the Gods of this rite and cleanse ourselves in preparation of to receive their blessings.  “

 

Wood pieces are passed to the people as the Priest speaks:

 

Lugus – Fire (Wood pieces*) –  “Father of Skill! Bright Shining One! Patron of Warriors, Craftsmen, and Leaders. We recognize your bright strength in the Sun above us and in the Fire before us. We ask that you grant us strength and vitality as we feed this flame.”

 

“Lugus, denos tu fuerza y energia! Accepta nuestra offrendas!”

 

“Lugus, give us your strength and energy! Accept the offerings from your people”

(All repeat)

 

“Teioureikis!”

 

 

Flowers are passed around to the people as the Priest speaks:

 

Nabia – Water (Flowers) – “Lady of flowing streams, raging rivers, and of the great sea that surrounds us. We feel your strength in the crashing waves and welcome your nurturing spirit in the waters we drink. Goddess of life sustaining waters. We ask that you cleanse us as we wash ourselves in the sea.”

 

“Nabia, renueve nuestras corazones y accepta estas offrendas!”

 

“Nabia, renew our hearts and accept our offerings!”

(All repeat)

 

“Teioureikis!”

 

 

The people will be instructed to offer their pieces of wood to the fire and touch the flame for cleansing. They will then take the flowers and offer them up to the sea in which they will wash their hands and return to the circle. Drummers will play a continuous drum beat along with other instruments.

 

Priest 1: “As is our tradition, a gift calls for a gift…..Let us prepare for the blessings of the Gods.”

 

“La Pega” (Bull Sacrifice)/Blessing Cup

 

The horn is blown, and the Barrosero (Bull Dancer*) is led into the circle by a Priest. The Barrosero is paraded around the fire 3 times. The people are led into rhythmic clapping. The Bull is released, after which the Barrosero does a “Baile de Toro” (Bull Dance*). The Bull struts and threatens to find its way out of the circle while the people continue to clap. As the Bull Dance reaches its peak, three Priests step out and perform the “Pega”.  Priest 1 faces off with the Bull and grabs the Bull by its horns. Two other Priests take either side of the Bull and help restrain it and force the dancer to his knees. Priest 1, using a falcata, punctures a bag attached to the Barrosero in which Sangria (symbolic of blood) is drained from and into the Blessing cup. (Priestess sings during the Bull sacrifice.)

 

Priestess: “Behold the Waters of Life!”

 

The Barrosero’s horns are removed and tossed into the flames. He is then given a cloak which to cover himself with. The Blessing Cup is passed around to the people, starting with the Barrosero.

 

(Note: The singing and clapping is intended to excite the Bull, which charges the waters of life! The priests and drummer must lead the people in this endeavor. The louder and stronger the music and sound, the more potent the waters!)

 

Priest 1: “We will now see what advice the Gods wish us to leave this place with…”

 

 

Omen

 

The unused portion of the blessing cup will be used for the Omen reading. It will be poured out three times and the spill patterns will be read to see what messages are to be received.
