Grove Attunement- Two Powers Meditation

 

Statement of Purpose

 

PD:  We gather tonight as a community to celebrate Samhain.  We have reached the most pivotal time of the year, when we are at the end, but also at the beginning of the cycle of the year.  We are at that most sacred point were death and life meet.  During the pre-Christian times for the Celts, the death of the last harvest and animals meant life by having enough sustenance to survive the Winter.  Obviously, we are fortunate to live in easier times, in the sense that we do not have to slaughter our own food.  However, we are still connected to the ancient ways.  In Celtic belief and tradition, everything begins in darkness.  All life, a seedling in the ground, a baby in her mother’s womb, gestates and grows in complete darkness. Every singe day, we see this idea in action.  How many people here can survive without sleep?  No one can.  We sleep in the darkness so we may rejuvenate and prepare for the next day’s activities.  

During this ending and beginning of the cycle, a holy union happens between The Morrigan and The Dagda.  The union between the Goddess of Death and the God of Abundance brings forth a renewed land, a rejuvenated land ready to rest during the winter and prepare for the plantings next Spring.  All of our holidays are interconnected.  Here we are half way around the wheel of the year from Beltaine, the joyous, time of romping in the fields.  Yet, a sacred mating, continues the cycle of life, just like at that holiday.

Also within this sacred turning towards the darkness, the veil becomes thin.  We can reach out and touch our loved ones on the other side.  We honor them and feast with them, so they know that they will never be forgotten.

Main Offering

 

The story of The Morrigan and The Dagda’s mating becomes one of the most vital myths in Celtic cosmology. 

 

While the Tuatha De Danaan battle the Formorians for control of the lands of Ireland, The Dagda is sent out on a scouting mission.  He comes upon a gorgeous woman with long red hair. (This is the Morrigan in one of her guises).  She is washing clothes at the ford of the Unshin River.  So, while The Dadga takes his scouting very seriously, he takes lovemaking much much more seriously, as any lusty God would!  They make love astride the ford, with the Morrigan placing one foot on each bank of the Unshin. 

After their mating, the Morrigan goes into a trance and sees the future.  She warns the Dagda about the Formorians actions.  She foresees the killing of the leader of the Formorians, and so the Tuatha De Danaan win the battle!

 

Within this myth, the connections between sex, war, death and life can be seen.  By mating, The Morrigan and The Dagda bestow sovereignty on the land.  Through the death of battle, the survival of the Tuatha De Danaan can thrive and grow.  Also, by making love on the land at the dark time of year, The Morrigan and The Dagda prepare the Earth for her resting time so that by Imbolc the seedlings will begin to grow.

 

So, in honor of our Deities of the Season, let’s reflect on those we have lost.  Let’s reflect on what in our own lives has died.  We honor and accept the cycle of death and life, as difficult as it is sometimes. Please come forward to the Cauldron at the Ancestors altar.  Please take an apple and say the name of a person you lost this past year. Whisper into the apple if you prefer.   Place it in the Cauldron.  The apple will go to the Otherworld and will then be reborn, as is the cycle of life.

Magical Working – Lighting our Fires from the Bonfire

 

PD:  Throughout our gathering, we have done work in the olde ways of The Celts.  We have followed the traditions of Samhain.  We have focused on Hospitality for the Dead.  We have focused on Sacrifice.  We have focused on Timelessness as we stand in between the worlds and time and space.  We have talked about the theme of renewal.  Let us now fully work with the theme of renewal by working with our ritual bonfire.

 

Fire burns away the old, transforms the old into new.  The moment a fire sparks, darkness ends and light begins.  Let us now, as our ancestors before, light our own fire and begin a new cycle, from the flame of our ritual bonfire.

 

[ Candles passed out to the folk ]

 

PD:  As I come around to light your flame, think about what you want new in the next year.  What will be renewed when your flame is lit?  Please state what your want new in your life. 

 

[ Steady, slow drumbeat only during working ]

 

PD:  Now that we have all lit our flames, you are renewed.  Please take your candle home with you and burn it knowing the magic of renewal is imbued within the candle from our ritual bonfire!
