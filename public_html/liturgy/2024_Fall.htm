Opening Script


Welcome
Welcome to Raven’s Cry Grove’s Norse celebration of the Fall Equinox.  A Haustblót  /HOOST-bloat/ is a time of sacrifice and worship.  It bears the theme of harvest and the inevitable laying to rest that the harvest cycle represents.  

Offerings for Harvest Home
You of course should make offerings to your Land Spirits. You can also make an offering again to Freyr Yngve  /FRAY-rar ING-vah/. Some might also decide to begin making offerings to Odin /OH-din/ in preparation for the dark season ahead.

Sharing Abundance
We talk a lot about “Hospitality” in Heathenry, but along with hospitality, we talk about generosity. Generosity is in sharing the good things in life, not just with those we love, but with those who need them even if we don’t know who they are. And as Freyr Yngve  /FRAY-rar ING-vah/ reminds us at the end of the Harvest: one day, like the grain, we too will be reaped and scattered. Death will someday come for us too, and whatever wealth we hoard will not stop it.

But in sharing abundance, in giving generously and living a generous life, we create memories and a reputation that is a blessing and a comfort.

At the break, those of you who wish to share of your bounty will have the opportunity to share canned goods for donation to a local food bank.

INITIATING THE RITE
 
Bunny: Druids! Welcome to the Norse Fall Equinox ritual Haustblót /HOOST-bloat/ - of Raven's Cry Grove, ADF!

As Summer comes to an end, the time of reaping the harvest is upon us. We have watched the seeds we planted in Spring grow and blossom through Summer, and now begin to bear fruit as the days grow shorter. Now the Harvest begins and we gather the fruit of our labor. For our Ancestors in the North, this meant harvesting foodstuffs to last through the harsh wintertime, and the need for abundance to last through that barren time.

The beginning of harvest is a time to honor Freyr Yngve /FRAY-rar ING-vah/, the keeper of Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, the land spirits and deities.  He is the giver of sunlight and rain and all that is needed for the crops to grow.  His great gift is ár ok friðr /AHR ok FREE/, peace and plenty.

Em:  Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling you, whispering on the wind. Release the cares of your mundane life. Take a deep breath in and allow your mind to be still. In this space we create our sacred realities. We can become all the Kindreds believe us to be. Come forward, be purified and attune yourself to mythic time. Let go and cross the divide.

Norse Haustblót Tradition:
Em: One tradition during the Haustblót /HOOST-bloat/ Fall Festival is to feast with the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/.    Freyr Yngve  /FRAY-rar ING-vah/ brings growth to the land, but only in the death of that which grows can he balance and protect us, providing us with that which sustains us.  He brings the abundance of harvest and sustenance for the winter, which is both practically and metaphorically the key to our survival. It is important we work in harmony with the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ in the actions we take throughout the year. But at this time, it is a time to celebrate! A time to feast and partake of the bounty of the year!

Bunny:  Honored Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, we share this bounty together in your honor as our Ancestors did
